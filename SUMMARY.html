Solution Overview

The goal of this project is to construct a real-time Voice Activity Detection (VAD) endpoint classifier. Our system must determine whether a pause in user audio represents a mid-turn breath (a HOLD) or if the speaker has finished their utterance (an EOT).

By default, voice interfaces rely on static silence timers. These baseline systems wait for a fixed, long duration (e.g., 1.6s) to ensure they do not cut off a user mid-sentence. Our machine learning solution analyzes the acoustic qualities of the speech leading up to the pause, allowing the voice assistant to take the floor in as little as 100 ms with less than 5% interruption rate.

Features and Signal Design

The feature extractor transforms the raw audio segment preceding the pause start (strictly causal, 0 to pause_start) into a dense representation of 19 linguistic and acoustic dimensions:

Pause Index Speaking Duration Pause Start Time F0 Pitch Mean F0 Pitch Std F0 Pitch Max/Min Final Voiced Slope RMS Energy Mean RMS Energy Std Final 0.5s Energy Mean Final 0.5s Energy Slope Zero Crossing Rate (ZCR) Mean ZCR Slope Spectral Centroid Mean Spectral Centroid Slope

Causal Logic: Using pause_end or any future frames violates causality and would result in an invalid model during live streaming. Our feature set relies heavily on structural turn features (such as dialog progress via pause_index and duration history) combined with short-term acoustic tendencies (such as slope of pitch and energy decay immediately before the silence boundary).

Model Architecture

Our code evaluates four distinct architectures inside a 5-fold GroupShuffleSplit (grouped by turn_id to prevent leakage across dialogue samples):

  1. Logistic Regression: Base linear model using balanced class weights.
  2. PyTorch MLP: 2 hidden layers (64 units) with ReLU activations.
  3. Random Forest: Ensemble model tracking non-linear interactions.
  4. HistGradientBoosting: Advanced gradient boosting classifier.

The system evaluates the CV scores and automatically picks the best architecture, calibrating outputs via Platt Scaling (CalibratedClassifierCV) to provide clean probability boundaries.

The Case Against Silence-Only

Why does our model beat VAD silence detectors?

"Dialogue is structured. A user takes breaths (holds) between clauses, which average 400-600ms in duration. If a system triggers on pure silence, it must wait at least 1.6s to avoid interrupting the user. By combining semantic turn tracking (pause indices) with physical cues (intonation fall and energy decay), we identify true turn ends immediately, cutting response latency by over 80%."
